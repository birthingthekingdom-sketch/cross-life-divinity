## ACCREDITATION & CREDENTIALS

### Q1: Is CLAC accreditation recognized by employers and other institutions?

**A:** CLAC (Cross Life Accreditation Council) is our internal quality assurance framework, not regional or national accreditation. However:
- We award **CPD (Continuing Professional Development) hours** which are recognized for professional development
- Our certificates demonstrate commitment to theological education
- We have a **Certificate Upgrade Promise**: If CLSD achieves national accreditation in the future, all prior certificates will be upgraded at no additional cost
- We maintain transfer pathways with select regionally accredited institutions

**Be Honest:** CLAC is not the same as regional accreditation from bodies like ATS (Association of Theological Schools). If you need regionally accredited credits for ordination or denominational requirements, please verify with your organization first.

---

### Q2: Will these courses count toward ordination requirements?

**A:** This depends on your denomination or ordaining body. Some key points:
- Many denominations accept CPD hours for continuing education requirements
- Some denominations have specific accreditation requirements (usually regional accreditation)
- Our courses provide the theological knowledge needed for ordination, but may not fulfill formal credit requirements
- **Recommendation:** Check with your denominational leadership before enrolling if ordination credits are your primary goal

**What CLSD IS good for:**
- Deepening theological knowledge
- Sermon preparation and teaching
- Personal spiritual growth
- Professional development (CPD hours)
- Credential enhancement (certificates)

---

### Q3: Can I transfer credits to a seminary or other institution?

**A:** We have established transfer pathways with select institutions, but this is evaluated on a case-by-case basis:
- **Contact us** with the name of the institution you're interested in transferring to
- We'll work with you to determine if a transfer pathway exists
- Some institutions may accept our courses for **elective credit** or **continuing education**
- **Regional accreditation** institutions typically don't accept credits from non-regionally accredited programs

**Alternative approach:** Use CLSD courses to **prepare** for seminary entrance exams or to **supplement** your seminary education at a fraction of the cost.
