# CCA Competencies Checklist

## Certified Chaplain Assistant (CCA) Core Competencies

This checklist outlines the essential competencies you'll develop through the Chaplaincy Training program. Use this as a guide to track your progress and prepare for CCA certification with the Association of Certified Christian Chaplains (ACCC).

---

## 1. Crisis Intervention and CISM

- [ ] Understand the phases of critical incident stress
- [ ] Recognize signs of acute stress reactions
- [ ] Apply crisis intervention techniques
- [ ] Provide immediate emotional support during emergencies
- [ ] Document crisis interventions appropriately
- [ ] Know when to refer to mental health professionals

**Key Skills:**
- Rapid assessment of crisis situations
- De-escalation techniques
- Peer support and debriefing
- Trauma-informed care

---

## 2. Spiritual First Aid and Pastoral Care

- [ ] Provide spiritual comfort to individuals in distress
- [ ] Conduct meaningful pastoral conversations
- [ ] Offer prayer and spiritual resources appropriately
- [ ] Support individuals through grief and loss
- [ ] Recognize spiritual crises and respond compassionately
- [ ] Maintain spiritual presence without imposing beliefs

**Key Skills:**
- Active listening and empathy
- Spiritual assessment
- Pastoral counseling techniques
- Interfaith pastoral care

---

## 3. Ethical Practice and Confidentiality

- [ ] Understand professional ethical standards for chaplains
- [ ] Maintain strict confidentiality and privacy
- [ ] Navigate legal and ethical dilemmas
- [ ] Recognize and prevent boundary violations
- [ ] Report abuse and safety concerns appropriately
- [ ] Practice informed consent in pastoral relationships

**Key Skills:**
- HIPAA compliance
- Mandatory reporting requirements
- Ethical decision-making frameworks
- Professional boundaries

---

## 4. Professional Boundaries

- [ ] Establish appropriate professional relationships
- [ ] Recognize dual relationships and conflicts of interest
- [ ] Maintain emotional objectivity
- [ ] Avoid over-involvement with individuals
- [ ] Set healthy limits on availability
- [ ] Manage transference and countertransference

**Key Skills:**
- Boundary setting
- Professional communication
- Self-awareness
- Conflict resolution

---

## 5. Interfaith and Cultural Sensitivity

- [ ] Respect diverse religious and spiritual traditions
- [ ] Understand cultural differences in grief and healing
- [ ] Provide culturally competent care
- [ ] Avoid imposing personal beliefs
- [ ] Learn about major world religions
- [ ] Collaborate with religious leaders from other traditions

**Key Skills:**
- Cultural humility
- Religious literacy
- Inclusive language
- Interfaith dialogue

---

## 6. Self-Care and Resilience

- [ ] Recognize signs of compassion fatigue and burnout
- [ ] Develop personal spiritual practices
- [ ] Maintain physical and mental health
- [ ] Seek supervision and support when needed
- [ ] Practice stress management techniques
- [ ] Build resilience through community and reflection

**Key Skills:**
- Stress management
- Personal wellness planning
- Peer support networks
- Reflective practice

---

## 7. Communication and Documentation

- [ ] Document pastoral encounters appropriately
- [ ] Communicate clearly with healthcare and institutional teams
- [ ] Write effective pastoral care notes
- [ ] Present information professionally
- [ ] Maintain accurate records
- [ ] Communicate with families and loved ones

**Key Skills:**
- Professional writing
- Team communication
- Electronic health records (EHR)
- Reporting and documentation

---

## 8. Institutional Settings Knowledge

- [ ] Understand hospital chaplaincy operations
- [ ] Know military chaplaincy protocols
- [ ] Understand correctional facility procedures
- [ ] Learn corporate chaplaincy best practices
- [ ] Navigate institutional hierarchies
- [ ] Work collaboratively with interdisciplinary teams

**Key Skills:**
- Institutional knowledge
- Team collaboration
- Protocol adherence
- Stakeholder communication

---

## 9. Spiritual Assessment and Care Planning

- [ ] Conduct spiritual assessments
- [ ] Identify spiritual needs and concerns
- [ ] Develop pastoral care plans
- [ ] Evaluate effectiveness of interventions
- [ ] Adjust care based on individual needs
- [ ] Involve individuals in their own care planning

**Key Skills:**
- Assessment tools
- Care planning
- Goal setting
- Outcome evaluation

---

## 10. Professional Development and Continuing Education

- [ ] Understand the path to full chaplain certification
- [ ] Know CPE (Clinical Pastoral Education) requirements
- [ ] Pursue ongoing professional development
- [ ] Stay current with best practices
- [ ] Participate in professional organizations
- [ ] Engage in reflective practice and supervision

**Key Skills:**
- Lifelong learning
- Professional networking
- Supervision and mentoring
- Career planning
