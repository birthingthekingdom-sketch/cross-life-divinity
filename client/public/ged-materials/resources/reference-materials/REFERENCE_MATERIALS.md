| Shape | Volume Formula | Surface Area Formula |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------|

| Sphere | V = (4/3)πr³ | SA = 4πr² |
| Cone | V = (1/3)πr²h | SA = πr² + πrl |

#### Other Important Formulas

**Distance:** d = rt (distance = rate × time)

**Simple Interest:** I = Prt (interest = principal × rate × time)

**Slope:** m = (y₂ - y₁)/(x₂ - x₁)

**Pythagorean Theorem:** a² + b² = c²

**Quadratic Formula:** x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a

**Mean (Average):** Mean = (sum of values) / (number of values)

**Probability:** P(event) = (number of favorable outcomes) / (total number of outcomes)

## Language Arts Reference Materials

### Grammar Rules

#### Subject-Verb Agreement
- Singular subject → singular verb
- Plural subject → plural verb
- Example: "The dog runs" (correct), "The dogs runs" (incorrect)

#### Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement
- Pronouns must agree with their antecedents in number and gender
- Example: "Each student should bring his or her book" (correct)

#### Verb Tenses
- **Present:** I walk, you walk, he/she walks
- **Past:** I walked, you walked, he/she walked
- **Future:** I will walk, you will walk, he/she will walk

#### Comma Usage
- Use commas to separate items in a list
- Use commas before coordinating conjunctions (and, but, or)
- Use commas after introductory phrases
- Use commas to set off nonessential information

#### Semicolon Usage
- Use semicolons to join independent clauses
- Use semicolons to separate items in a list with commas

### Common Writing Errors

| Error | Example | Correction |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Run-on sentence | "I went to the store I bought milk." | "I went to the store. I bought milk." or "I went to the store and bought milk." |
| Fragment | "Because it was raining." | "I stayed inside because it was raining." |
| Misplaced modifier | "Running down the street, the ball hit me." | "Running down the street, I was hit by the ball." |
| Dangling modifier | "After finishing dinner, the movie started." | "After we finished dinner, the movie started." |

### Transition Words

**To show addition:** also, furthermore, in addition, moreover

**To show contrast:** however, on the other hand, nevertheless, in contrast

**To show cause and effect:** because, as a result, consequently, therefore

**To show sequence:** first, second, next, finally, then

**To show emphasis:** indeed, in fact, certainly, definitely

### Scientific Method Steps

1. **Observation:** Make observations about the world
2. **Question:** Ask a question about what you observed
3. **Hypothesis:** Make a prediction about the answer
4. **Experiment:** Design and conduct an experiment to test your hypothesis
5. **Analysis:** Analyze the data from your experiment
6. **Conclusion:** Draw conclusions based on your analysis

### Key Science Concepts

#### Biology
- **Photosynthesis:** 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
- **Cellular Respiration:** C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + energy
- **DNA Structure:** Double helix with base pairs (A-T, G-C)

#### Chemistry
- **Atomic Structure:** Protons (positive), neutrons (neutral), electrons (negative)
- **Chemical Bonds:** Ionic, covalent, metallic
- **pH Scale:** 0-14 (7 is neutral, <7 is acidic, >7 is basic)

#### Physics
- **Newton's Laws:**
  1. An object at rest stays at rest unless acted upon by a force
  2. F = ma (force = mass × acceleration)
  3. For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction

#### Earth Science
- **Rock Cycle:** Igneous → Sedimentary → Metamorphic → Igneous
- **Water Cycle:** Evaporation → Condensation → Precipitation → Collection
- **Plate Tectonics:** Earth's crust divided into moving plates

### Data Interpretation

#### Reading Graphs
- **Title:** What does the graph show?
- **Axes:** What variables are being measured?
- **Scale:** What are the units and intervals?
- **Trend:** What pattern does the data show?

#### Reading Tables
- **Headers:** What do the columns represent?
- **Data:** What values are shown?
- **Relationships:** How do the variables relate?

## Study Tips for Reference Materials

### Before Test Day
- Memorize all formulas provided on the formula sheet
- Practice using reference materials during practice tests
- Understand how to interpret graphs, tables, and charts
- Review historical dates and events
- Learn periodic table elements

### During Test Day
- Use reference materials efficiently
- Don't waste time looking up information you should know
- Focus on understanding questions, not just finding answers
- Use reference materials to verify your answers
