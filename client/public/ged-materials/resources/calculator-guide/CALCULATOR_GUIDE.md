# GED Calculator Guide

## Overview

The GED Math test allows calculator use for Part 2 (41 questions). Understanding how to use the calculator effectively can significantly improve your test performance. This guide covers calculator basics, functions, and strategies.

---

## Calculator Information

### Calculator Type
The GED uses the **TI-30XS MultiView** calculator, which is provided at the testing center. You cannot bring your own calculator.

### Calculator Features
- **Display:** 4-line display showing calculations and results
- **Functions:** Basic arithmetic, fractions, decimals, percentages, powers, roots
- **Memory:** Can store values for later use
- **Modes:** Standard, scientific, and programming modes
- **Buttons:** Approximately 50 buttons for different functions

## Basic Calculator Operations

### Arithmetic Operations

**Addition:** 5 + 3 = 8
- Press: 5 + 3 =

**Subtraction:** 10 - 4 = 6
- Press: 10 - 4 =

**Multiplication:** 6 × 7 = 42
- Press: 6 × 7 =

**Division:** 20 ÷ 4 = 5
- Press: 20 ÷ 4 =

### Order of Operations
The calculator follows the standard order of operations (PEMDAS):
1. Parentheses
2. Exponents
3. Multiplication and Division (left to right)
4. Addition and Subtraction (left to right)

**Example:** 2 + 3 × 4 = 14 (not 20)
- Press: 2 + 3 × 4 =
- Result: 14

### Using Parentheses
Parentheses change the order of operations:

**Example:** (2 + 3) × 4 = 20
- Press: ( 2 + 3 ) × 4 =
- Result: 20

## Working with Fractions

### Entering Fractions
**Example:** 3/4
- Press: 3 ÷ 4 =
- Result: 0.75

### Adding Fractions
**Example:** 1/2 + 1/4 = 3/4
- Press: 1 ÷ 2 + 1 ÷ 4 =
- Result: 0.75 (which equals 3/4)

### Multiplying Fractions
**Example:** 2/3 × 3/4 = 1/2
- Press: 2 ÷ 3 × 3 ÷ 4 =
- Result: 0.5 (which equals 1/2)

### Dividing Fractions
**Example:** 1/2 ÷ 1/4 = 2
- Press: 1 ÷ 2 ÷ 1 ÷ 4 =
- Result: 2

## Memory Functions

### Storing a Value
**Example:** Store 25 in memory
- Press: 25 STO (or M+)
- The calculator displays "M" to show memory is active

### Recalling a Value
**Example:** Recall the stored value
- Press: RCL (or M)
- Result: 25

### Clearing Memory
**Example:** Clear memory
- Press: CLR (or DEL)
- The "M" indicator disappears

### Using Stored Values
**Example:** Add 10 to stored value (25 + 10 = 35)
- Press: RCL + 10 =
- Result: 35

## Common GED Math Problems

### Problem 1: Calculating Area
**Example:** Find the area of a rectangle with length 8 and width 5
- Formula: Area = length × width
- Press: 8 × 5 =
- Result: 40 square units

### Problem 2: Calculating Perimeter
**Example:** Find the perimeter of a rectangle with length 8 and width 5
- Formula: Perimeter = 2(length + width)
- Press: 2 × ( 8 + 5 ) =
- Result: 26 units

### Problem 3: Solving Equations
**Example:** Solve 3x + 5 = 20
- Rearrange: 3x = 15, so x = 15 ÷ 3
- Press: 15 ÷ 3 =
- Result: 5

### Problem 4: Calculating Average
**Example:** Find the average of 85, 90, 78, 92
- Formula: Average = (85 + 90 + 78 + 92) ÷ 4
- Press: ( 85 + 90 + 78 + 92 ) ÷ 4 =
- Result: 86.25

### Problem 5: Calculating Percent
**Example:** What is 15% of 200?
- Formula: 0.15 × 200
- Press: 0.15 × 200 =
- Result: 30

## When to Use the Calculator

### Use Calculator For:
- Complex arithmetic calculations
- Multi-step problems
- Decimal and fraction calculations
- Percentage calculations
- Square roots and powers
- Checking your work

### Don't Use Calculator For:
- Simple mental math (2 + 3)
- Reading and understanding the problem
- Setting up the equation
- Interpreting the result
- Checking if your answer makes sense

## Calculator Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Wrong Order of Operations
**Problem:** Calculating 2 + 3 × 4
- **Wrong:** 2 + 3 = 5, then 5 × 4 = 20
- **Right:** 3 × 4 = 12, then 2 + 12 = 14
- **Solution:** Use parentheses to ensure correct order

### Mistake 2: Forgetting Parentheses
**Problem:** Calculating (2 + 3) × 4
- **Wrong:** 2 + 3 × 4 = 14
- **Right:** (2 + 3) × 4 = 20
- **Solution:** Always use parentheses for grouped operations

### Mistake 3: Decimal Point Errors
**Problem:** Entering 3.5 as 35
- **Wrong:** 35 + 2 = 37
- **Right:** 3.5 + 2 = 5.5
- **Solution:** Double-check decimal point placement

### Mistake 4: Forgetting to Clear
**Problem:** Previous calculation affects new calculation
- **Wrong:** 5 + 3 = 8, then press × 2 = 16 (instead of 10)
- **Right:** Clear calculator before new calculation
- **Solution:** Press CLR or DEL before starting new problem

### Mistake 5: Misinterpreting Results
**Problem:** Calculator shows 0.333333... but you need a fraction
- **Wrong:** Writing 0.333333 as the answer
- **Right:** Recognizing this as 1/3
- **Solution:** Understand what the decimal represents

## Calculator Tips for Test Day

### Before the Test
- Familiarize yourself with the calculator
- Practice using it with sample problems
- Know where all the buttons are
- Understand how to clear and reset

### During the Test
- Use the calculator for complex calculations
- Double-check your entries before pressing =
- Verify that your result makes sense
- Use scratch paper to track your work

### After Getting Your Result
- Check if your answer is reasonable
- Verify decimal placement
- Confirm units (dollars, inches, etc.)
- Use calculator to verify your work

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I bring my own calculator?**  
A: No, the testing center provides a TI-30XS MultiView calculator.

**Q: What if the calculator breaks during the test?**  
A: Alert the proctor immediately. They will provide a replacement.

**Q: Can I use the calculator for the first part of the Math test?**  
A: No, the first 5 questions must be answered without a calculator.

**Q: Should I use the calculator for every problem?**  
A: No, use it for complex calculations. Simple mental math is faster.

**Q: What if I'm not comfortable with the calculator?**  
A: Practice with sample problems before the test. The testing center may allow extra time to familiarize yourself.
