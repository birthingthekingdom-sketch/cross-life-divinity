# GED Actual Exam Structure and Guidelines

## Overview

The actual GED exam is a computer-based test administered at authorized testing centers. This guide provides comprehensive information about the exam format, scoring, registration, and test-day procedures.

---

## Exam Structure

### Four Separate Tests

The GED consists of four separate subject tests that can be taken on the same day or on different days:

| Subject | Duration | Format | Questions | Passing Score |
|---------|----------|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Mathematical Reasoning | 120 minutes | Multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank | 46 | 145/200 |
| Reasoning Through Language Arts | 150 minutes | Multiple choice, short answer, essay | 51 | 145/200 |
| Science | 90 minutes | Multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank | 34 | 145/200 |
| Social Studies | 90 minutes | Multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank | 35 | 145/200 |

**Total Test Time:** 450 minutes (7.5 hours) if all four tests taken in one day

---

## Mathematical Reasoning Test (120 minutes)

### Test Format
The Math test has two parts:

**Part 1 (No Calculator):** 5 questions, 5 minutes  
**Part 2 (Calculator Allowed):** 41 questions, 115 minutes

### Content Areas
- **Arithmetic:** 20-30% (number operations, fractions, decimals, percentages)
- **Algebra:** 30-40% (equations, functions, linear equations, inequalities)
- **Geometry:** 20-30% (shapes, area, volume, coordinate geometry)
- **Data Analysis:** 15-20% (statistics, probability, graphs, charts)

### Question Types
- **Multiple Choice:** Select one correct answer from four options
- **Fill-in-the-Blank:** Enter a numerical answer
- **Drag-and-Drop:** Select and arrange items
- **Hot Spot:** Click on correct location in image

### Scoring
- **Raw Score:** Number of correct answers (0-46)
- **Scaled Score:** 100-200 points
- **Passing Score:** 145 points

---

## Reasoning Through Language Arts Test (150 minutes)

### Test Format
The Language Arts test has three parts:

**Part 1 (Reading):** 14 questions, 35 minutes  
**Part 2 (Grammar):** 34 questions, 75 minutes  
**Part 3 (Writing):** 3 questions, 40 minutes

### Content Areas
- **Reading Comprehension:** 40-50% (fiction, non-fiction, technical texts)
- **Grammar and Mechanics:** 30-40% (sentence structure, punctuation, word choice)
- **Writing:** 15-20% (essay writing, organization, development)

### Question Types
- **Multiple Choice:** Select one correct answer
- **Drag-and-Drop:** Arrange sentences or correct errors
- **Short Answer:** Write 1-3 sentences
- **Extended Response:** Write a 3-5 paragraph essay

### Scoring
- **Raw Score:** Points based on question type (0-51)
- **Scaled Score:** 100-200 points
- **Passing Score:** 145 points
- **Essay Scoring:** 0-4 points based on rubric (organization, development, conventions)

---

## Science Test (90 minutes)

### Test Format
The Science test is one continuous section with 34 questions in 90 minutes.

### Content Areas
- **Life Science:** 40-45% (biology, human body, genetics, ecology)
- **Physical Science:** 40-45% (chemistry, physics, energy, motion)
- **Earth and Space Science:** 10-15% (geology, astronomy, weather)

### Question Types
- **Multiple Choice:** Select one correct answer
- **Fill-in-the-Blank:** Enter a numerical answer
- **Drag-and-Drop:** Match items or arrange sequences
- **Hot Spot:** Click on correct location in diagram

### Scoring
- **Raw Score:** Number of correct answers (0-34)
- **Scaled Score:** 100-200 points
- **Passing Score:** 145 points

---

## Social Studies Test (90 minutes)

### Test Format
The Social Studies test is one continuous section with 35 questions in 90 minutes.

### Content Areas
- **U.S. History:** 35-40% (colonial period through modern era)
- **Civics and Government:** 25-30% (Constitution, branches, rights, responsibilities)
- **Economics:** 15-20% (supply/demand, trade, financial systems)
- **Geography:** 10-15% (maps, regions, human-environment interaction)
- **World History:** 15-20% (major civilizations, events, cultures)

### Question Types
- **Multiple Choice:** Select one correct answer
- **Fill-in-the-Blank:** Enter a numerical or short text answer
- **Drag-and-Drop:** Match items or arrange sequences
- **Hot Spot:** Click on correct location in map or image

### Scoring
- **Raw Score:** Number of correct answers (0-35)
- **Scaled Score:** 100-200 points
- **Passing Score:** 145 points

---

## Overall Scoring

### Scaled Scores
Each subject is scored on a scale of 100-200 points. The GED score is based on scaled scores, not raw scores.

### Passing Requirements
- **Minimum per subject:** 145 points
- **Total minimum:** 580 points (all four subjects)
- **To pass GED:** Must pass all four subjects

### Score Levels

**Below 145:** Not Passed  
**145-164:** Passed  
**165-174:** Passed with Honors  
**175+:** Passed with High Honors  

### Score Report
Your official GED score report includes:
- Scaled score for each subject
- Overall GED status (Passed/Not Passed)
- Percentile ranking
- Performance level for each subject
- Diagnostic feedback for improvement areas

## Test-Taking Environment

### Testing Center Requirements
- **Location:** Authorized GED testing centers (schools, libraries, community colleges)
- **Equipment:** Computer with keyboard and mouse
- **Proctoring:** Test is proctored by trained administrator
- **Security:** Photo ID required, no personal items allowed

### Allowed Materials
- **Provided:** Calculator (for Math Part 2 only), formula sheet (Math), scratch paper
- **Not Allowed:** Phone, smartwatch, notes, textbooks, unauthorized calculators

### Breaks
- **Between Subjects:** 10-minute break (can extend to 30 minutes between tests)
- **During Test:** No breaks allowed within a subject test
- **Lunch Break:** Available if taking multiple tests in one day

### Accessibility Accommodations
- **Extended Time:** Up to 50% additional time for qualified students
- **Large Print:** Tests available in large print format
- **Audio:** Audio versions available for certain sections
- **Assistive Technology:** Screen readers and other assistive devices allowed

## Registration and Scheduling

### How to Register
1. Visit official GED testing website
2. Create account and verify email
3. Select testing center and date
4. Choose which subjects to test
5. Pay registration fee
6. Receive confirmation email

### Registration Deadlines
- **Standard Registration:** 7 days before test date
- **Late Registration:** 3 days before test date (additional fee)
- **Same-Day Registration:** Not available

### Testing Fees
- **Per Subject:** $30-35
- **All Four Subjects:** $120-140
- **Retake Fee:** Same as initial test fee

### Rescheduling and Cancellation
- **Reschedule:** Free if done 7+ days before test date
- **Cancellation:** Full refund if cancelled 7+ days before test date
- **Late Changes:** May incur additional fees

## Test-Day Procedures

### Before Test Day
- **Confirm Details:** Check confirmation email for date, time, location
- **Plan Route:** Know how to get to testing center
- **Prepare ID:** Bring valid photo ID (driver's license, passport)
- **Arrive Early:** Plan to arrive 15 minutes before test time
- **Review Materials:** Review practice tests and study guides

### Arrival at Testing Center
1. Arrive 15 minutes early
2. Check in at front desk
3. Present photo ID
4. Sign security agreement
5. Receive test instructions
6. Proceed to testing room

### During Test
- **Follow Instructions:** Listen carefully to proctor instructions
- **Manage Time:** Use timer to pace yourself
- **Read Carefully:** Read all questions thoroughly
- **Answer All Questions:** Unanswered questions count as wrong
- **Use Scratch Paper:** Work out math problems on scratch paper
- **Ask for Help:** Raise hand if you have questions about test procedures

### After Test
1. Review answers if time permits
2. Submit test when finished
3. Receive preliminary score (for some subjects)
4. Receive receipt with confirmation number
5. Official score report sent within 24 hours

## Score Improvement

### If You Don't Pass
- **Retake Options:** Can retake any subject you didn't pass
- **Waiting Period:** No waiting period between retakes
- **Practice:** Use practice tests to identify weak areas
- **Study:** Focus on topics where you scored lowest

### If You Pass
- **Official Diploma:** GED diploma sent within 2-3 weeks
- **Digital Badge:** Shareable credential available immediately
- **Transcript:** Official score report for college applications
- **Next Steps:** Use GED for college admission or employment

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is a passing GED score?**  
A: You must score at least 145 points on each of the four subjects. Your total score must be at least 580 points.

**Q: How long are GED scores valid?**  
A: GED scores are valid for life. They never expire.

**Q: Can I take the GED on a computer at home?**  
A: No, the GED must be taken at an authorized testing center on a computer provided by the center.

**Q: How many times can I retake the GED?**  
A: You can retake the GED as many times as needed. There is no limit on retakes.

**Q: What if I need accommodations?**  
A: Contact the testing center about accommodations. You may need to provide documentation of disability.

**Q: When will I get my scores?**  
A: Preliminary scores are available immediately after testing. Official scores are sent within 24 hours.
