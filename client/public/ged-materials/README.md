## Program Overview

### Four GED Subjects

#### 1. 📐 Mathematical Reasoning (120 minutes)
- **Topics:** Arithmetic, algebra, geometry, data analysis, probability
- **Questions:** 46 (5 without calculator, 41 with calculator)
- **Passing Score:** 145/200
- **Study Guide:** [Math Study Guide](./study-guides/math/)
- **Practice Tests:** [Math Practice Tests](./practice-tests/math/)

#### 2. 📖 Reasoning Through Language Arts (150 minutes)
- **Topics:** Reading comprehension, grammar, writing, essays
- **Questions:** 51 (reading, grammar, writing)
- **Passing Score:** 145/200
- **Study Guide:** [Language Arts Study Guide](./study-guides/language-arts/)
- **Practice Tests:** [Language Arts Practice Tests](./practice-tests/language-arts/)

#### 3. 🔬 Science (90 minutes)
- **Topics:** Life science, physical science, earth science, scientific method
- **Questions:** 34 (multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank)
- **Passing Score:** 145/200
- **Study Guide:** [Science Study Guide](./study-guides/science/)
- **Practice Tests:** [Science Practice Tests](./practice-tests/science/)

#### 4. 🌍 Social Studies (90 minutes)
- **Topics:** U.S. history, civics, economics, geography, world history
- **Questions:** 35 (multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank)
- **Passing Score:** 145/200
- **Study Guide:** [Social Studies Study Guide](./study-guides/social-studies/)
- **Practice Tests:** [Social Studies Practice Tests](./practice-tests/social-studies/)

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Take a Diagnostic Test
Start by taking a diagnostic test to assess your current level in each subject. This helps you identify your strengths and weaknesses.

**Time Required:** 4-5 hours (all four subjects)  
**Location:** [Diagnostic Tests](./practice-tests/)

### Step 2: Review Your Results
Analyze your diagnostic test results to understand which subjects need the most attention.

**What to Look For:**
- Which subjects scored highest?
- Which subjects scored lowest?
- What topics are most challenging?
- What topics are your strengths?

### Step 3: Create Your Study Plan
Based on your diagnostic results, create a personalized study plan.

**Study Timeline Options:**
- **8-Week Intensive:** 15 hours/week (120 total hours)
- **12-Week Standard:** 13 hours/week (156 total hours)
- **16-Week Extended:** 10 hours/week (160 total hours)

**See:** [Time Management Guide](./resources/time-management/TIME_MANAGEMENT.md)

### Step 4: Study Using Our Materials
Follow your study plan using our comprehensive study guides and practice tests.

**Study Process:**
1. Read concept explanations in study guides
2. Review worked examples
3. Solve practice problems
4. Take practice tests to check progress
5. Review mistakes and weak areas

### Step 5: Practice with Unlimited Tests
Take unlimited practice tests to build confidence and identify remaining weak areas.

**Practice Test Features:**
- Unlimited attempts
- Immediate feedback
- Progress tracking
- Difficulty levels (Basic, Intermediate, Advanced)
- Full-length and short quizzes

**See:** [Practice Test Guide](./practice-tests/PRACTICE_TEST_GUIDE.md)

### Step 6: Prepare for Test Day
Use our test-taking strategies and time management tips to prepare for the actual exam.

**Final Preparation:**
- Take full-length practice tests
- Simulate test-day conditions
- Review test-taking strategies
- Practice time management
- Build confidence

**See:** [Test-Taking Strategies](./resources/test-strategies/TEST_STRATEGIES.md)

### Step 7: Register and Take the Exam
Register for the official GED exam at an authorized testing center.

**Registration:**
- Visit www.ged.com
- Select testing center and date
- Pay registration fee ($120-140 for all four subjects)
- Receive confirmation email

**See:** [Actual Exam Guide](./actual-exam/ACTUAL_EXAM_GUIDE.md)

## Study Tips

### Create a Study Schedule
- Plan 1-2 hours of study per day
- Study 5-6 days per week
- Take one day off for rest
- Adjust based on your baseline score

### Mix All Subjects
- Study different subjects on different days
- This helps maintain interest and prevents burnout
- It also helps integrate knowledge across subjects

### Focus on Weak Areas
- Identify topics where you score lowest
- Spend extra study time on these topics
- Retake practice tests to measure improvement

### Review Mistakes
- Don't just check your score
- Read explanations for all questions
- Understand why you got questions wrong
- Learn from every mistake

### Practice Regularly
- Take at least one practice test per week
- Gradually increase difficulty level
- Take full-length practice tests closer to test day
- Simulate test-day conditions

### Stay Motivated
- Track your progress
- Celebrate small improvements
- Join a study group
- Remember your long-term goal

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does it take to prepare for the GED?**  
A: Most students need 8-12 weeks of study. Your timeline depends on your current level and how much you study per week.

**Q: Can I take the GED on a computer at home?**  
A: No, the GED must be taken at an authorized testing center on a computer provided by the center.

**Q: How many times can I retake the GED?**  
A: You can retake the GED as many times as needed. There is no limit on retakes.

**Q: What is a passing GED score?**  
A: You must score at least 145 points on each of the four subjects. Your total score must be at least 580 points.

**Q: How long are GED scores valid?**  
A: GED scores are valid for life. They never expire.

**Q: Can I use a calculator on all math questions?**  
A: No, you cannot use a calculator on the first 5 questions of the Math test. A calculator is provided for the remaining 41 questions.

**Q: What materials should I bring to the test?**  
A: Bring a valid photo ID (driver's license or passport). Everything else is provided by the testing center.

**Q: What if I need accommodations?**  
A: Contact the testing center about accommodations. You may need to provide documentation of disability.
