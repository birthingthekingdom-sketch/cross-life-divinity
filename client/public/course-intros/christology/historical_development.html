Historical Development of Christology

Early Church & Councils

Council of Nicaea (325)

Affirmed Christ's full divinity and consubstantiality with the Father against Arianism.

Council of Chalcedon (451)

Defined Christ as one person in two natures (divine and human) without confusion or division.

Medieval Theology

Anselm's Atonement

Developed satisfaction theory explaining Christ's death as payment for human sin.

Aquinas on Incarnation

Synthesized Aristotelian philosophy with Christology, emphasizing Christ's role as mediator.

Reformation Era

Luther & Calvin

Emphasized Christ's work of redemption and the centrality of faith in Christ for salvation.

Substitutionary Atonement

Developed understanding of Christ's death as substitutionary sacrifice for human sin.

Modern & Contemporary

Liberal & Neo-Orthodox

Barth and others reasserted classical Christology against liberal reductionism.

Contextual Christologies

Feminist, liberation, and global perspectives enrich understanding of Christ's significance.
