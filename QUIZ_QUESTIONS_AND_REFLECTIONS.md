Omniscience means God has complete knowledge of all things, past, present, and future.

Which of the following is an incommunicable attribute?
- A) Love
- B) Wisdom
- C) Omnipotence ✓
- D) Mercy

**Question 5 (Short Answer)**
What is the relationship between God's transcendence and immanence?
- **Expected Answer**: Transcendence refers to God's absolute otherness and superiority over creation, while immanence refers to God's presence and involvement within creation. Both are essential to understanding God's nature.

**Question 6 (Multiple Choice)**
What does holiness mean in the context of God's attributes?
- A) God's ability to perform miracles

- C) God's power over nature

Name three communicable attributes of God and explain why they are called "communicable."
- **Expected Answer**: Communicable attributes include love, wisdom, justice, mercy, and faithfulness. They are called communicable because humans, made in God's image, can reflect these attributes in limited ways.

**Question 8 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the Trinity?
- A) Three separate gods
- B) God is one in essence but three in person: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit ✓
- C) Only the Father is God
- D) A mystery that cannot be understood at all

According to Matthew 28:19, what evidence does Scripture provide for the Trinity?
- **Expected Answer**: The baptismal formula invoking Father, Son, and Holy Spirit demonstrates the three persons of the Godhead.

**Question 10 (True/False)**
God's justice and mercy are contradictory attributes that cannot coexist.
- **Answer**: False ✓

**Question 11 (Short Answer)**
How does understanding God's holiness affect how believers should live?
- **Expected Answer**: Understanding God's holiness calls believers to pursue sanctification, seek righteousness, and separate themselves from sin.

**Question 12 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the primary implication of God's faithfulness for believers?
- A) God will punish us for our sins
- B) God will never leave us or forsake us ✓
- C) God will make us wealthy
- D) God will prevent all suffering

**Question 1**: Which attribute of God means the most to you personally and why? How has this attribute influenced your faith journey?

**Question 2**: How does understanding both God's transcendence and immanence affect your prayer life and your relationship with God?

#### Written Assignment

**Personal Prayer Reflection (1-2 pages)**

Compose a personal prayer that reflects on God's attributes and how they relate to your current circumstances or challenges. Your prayer should:

1. Address God directly, acknowledging specific attributes
2. Reflect on how these attributes provide comfort, guidance, or correction
3. Include a commitment to respond to God's character with obedience and worship
4. Be written in your own authentic voice and style

### Lesson 3: The Doctrine of Scripture

#### Quiz Questions (12 Total)

**Question 1 (Multiple Choice)**
What does the doctrine of Scripture address?
- A) The nature, origin, authority, and reliability of the Bible ✓
- B) Only the historical accuracy of biblical events
- C) The literary style of biblical authors
- D) The number of books in the Bible

**Question 2 (Multiple Choice)**
What does "God-breathed" (2 Timothy 3:16) mean?
- A) God dictated every word to scribes
- B) The Holy Spirit guided human authors so Scripture is God's authoritative Word ✓
- C) God only inspired the main ideas
- D) Scripture is merely human wisdom

**Question 3 (True/False)**
Verbal inspiration means that the very words of Scripture, not just the ideas, are inspired by God.
- **Answer**: True ✓

**Question 4 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the canon of Scripture?
- A) The collection of books recognized as Scripture ✓
- B) The rules for interpreting Scripture
- C) The historical period when Scripture was written
- D) The different translations of the Bible

**Question 5 (Short Answer)**
Name three criteria used by the early Church to determine which books belonged in the New Testament canon.
- **Expected Answer**: Apostolic authority or connection, doctrinal consistency with established Scripture, and universal acceptance by the early Church.

**Question 6 (Multiple Choice)**
What is hermeneutics?
- A) The study of biblical history
- B) The science of biblical interpretation ✓
- C) The translation of Scripture
- D) The memorization of Scripture

**Question 7 (Short Answer)**
Describe the grammatical-historical method of biblical interpretation.
- **Expected Answer**: This method interprets Scripture according to its normal grammatical meaning in its historical context, considering the original languages, cultural background, and literary context.

**Question 8 (Multiple Choice)**
What does "literal interpretation" mean in biblical hermeneutics?
- A) Taking every word exactly as written without any figures of speech
- B) Taking Scripture at face value while recognizing figures of speech and literary devices ✓
- C) Interpreting Scripture based only on personal feelings
- D) Ignoring the historical context

**Question 9 (Short Answer)**
What is the relationship between contextual interpretation and theological interpretation?
- **Expected Answer**: Contextual interpretation examines passages within their immediate and broader biblical context, while theological interpretation understands passages in light of Scripture's overall theological message. Both are essential for proper understanding.

**Question 10 (True/False)**
Scripture is sufficient for all matters of faith and practice, though it may not address every possible question.
- **Answer**: True ✓

**Question 11 (Short Answer)**
What does it mean that Scripture is "clear" or has clarity?
- **Expected Answer**: While some passages are difficult, the essential teachings of Scripture are clear enough for ordinary believers to understand and apply to their lives.

**Question 12 (Multiple Choice)**
Which of the following is NOT a principle of the grammatical-historical method?
- A) Consider the original languages
- B) Understand cultural background
- C) Interpret based on personal preferences ✓
- D) Examine literary genre

#### Written Reflection Questions (2)

**Question 1**: How do you currently approach reading and interpreting Scripture? What changes might you make based on what you've learned about hermeneutics?

**Question 2**: How does understanding the doctrine of Scripture's inspiration and authority affect your trust in the Bible and your obedience to its teachings?

#### Written Assignment

**Hermeneutical Analysis (2 pages)**

Choose a biblical passage of 3-5 verses and apply the grammatical-historical hermeneutical method. Your analysis should include:

1. **Historical and Cultural Context**: What was the historical situation? Who was the original audience? What cultural factors are relevant?
2. **Grammatical Analysis**: Define key terms. Identify important grammatical structures. Note verb tenses and moods.
3. **Literary Genre and Structure**: What type of literature is this? How does the passage fit within its broader context?
4. **Theological Significance**: What theological truth does this passage teach? How does it relate to other Scripture?
5. **Application to Contemporary Christian Life**: How should this passage shape how believers think and live today?

**Question 5 (True/False)**
Jesus experienced hunger, thirst, and fatigue during His earthly ministry.

Which attribute of God did Jesus NOT possess?
- A) Omniscience
- B) Omnipotence
- C) Eternality
- D) Limited knowledge ✓

What does it mean that Jesus was "sinless"?
- **Expected Answer**: Jesus was tempted in every way but did not sin. He was morally perfect and without any transgression against God's law.

**Question 8 (Multiple Choice)**
According to the lesson, what is the primary purpose of the incarnation?
- A) To accomplish redemption through His death and resurrection ✓
- B) To become a political leader
- C) To establish a new religion
- D) To demonstrate human weakness

**Question 9 (Short Answer)**
What does the title "Logos" (the Word) emphasize about Christ?
- **Expected Answer**: Christ as God's self-revelation and the eternal Word of God through whom all things were created.

**Question 10 (True/False)**
The hypostatic union means Jesus is two separate persons.
- **Answer**: False ✓

**Question 11 (Short Answer)**
How does Christ's sacrifice have infinite value?
- **Expected Answer**: Because God Himself died for humanity. The divine nature of Christ gives infinite worth to His sacrifice, making it sufficient for the sins of all people for all time.

**Question 12 (Multiple Choice)**

- A) He bridges the gap between holy God and sinful humanity ✓
- B) He eliminates the need for God

#### Written Assignment

**Essay: The Significance of the Incarnation (3 pages)**

Write a comprehensive essay exploring why the incarnation is central to Christian faith. Your essay should address:

1. **How the Incarnation Reveals God's Character**: Discuss what the incarnation reveals about God's nature, His love for humanity, and His commitment to redemption.

2. **Why God Becoming Human Was Necessary for Redemption**: Explain why God could not have redeemed humanity without becoming human. Consider the problem of sin, God's justice, and the need for a perfect sacrifice.

3. **How Understanding Christ's Two Natures Affects Worship and Obedience**: Reflect on how the reality of Christ's two natures should shape how you worship Him and live in obedience to His teachings.

4. **Personal Application**: Conclude with how understanding the incarnation has deepened your faith and commitment to Christ.

### Lesson 2: The Work of Christ: Atonement

#### Quiz Questions (12 Total)

**Question 1 (Multiple Choice)**
What does "atonement" refer to?
- A) Christ's reconciliation of sinful humanity with holy God through His death and resurrection ✓
- B) Christ's teachings about morality
- C) Christ's miracles and healings
- D) Christ's establishment of the Church

**Question 2 (Short Answer)**
What is the fundamental problem that the atonement addresses?
- **Expected Answer**: All humans are sinners who have fallen short of God's glory. Sin creates a barrier between humanity and God, resulting in spiritual death and separation from God.

**Question 3 (Multiple Choice)**
According to the substitutionary/penal theory of atonement, Christ:
- A) Bore the penalty for our sins in our place ✓
- B) Taught us how to be good
- C) Defeated Satan through military conquest
- D) Established a new moral code

**Question 4 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the Christus Victor theory of atonement?
- A) Christ defeated Satan, sin, and death through His resurrection ✓
- B) Christ's death was merely a moral example
- C) Christ satisfied God's honor
- D) Christ paid a ransom to the devil

**Question 5 (Short Answer)**
What is the moral influence theory of atonement?
- **Expected Answer**: Christ's death demonstrates God's love and moral character, inspiring us to repentance and transformation. While this element is important, it is insufficient as a complete explanation of atonement.

**Question 6 (Multiple Choice)**
What does the satisfaction theory of atonement emphasize?
- A) Christ's death satisfies God's honor and justice, restoring the proper relationship between God and humanity ✓
- B) Christ's death is merely symbolic
- C) Christ's death was unnecessary
- D) Christ's death benefits only a few people

**Question 7 (Short Answer)**
What is the significance of Christ's resurrection for the atonement?
- **Expected Answer**: The resurrection validates Christ's claims and work, demonstrates God's power over sin and death, provides the basis for our hope and future resurrection, and enables Christ's ongoing intercession for believers.

**Question 8 (Multiple Choice)**
What is Christ's exaltation?
- A) His ascension to heaven and session at God's right hand where He reigns as Lord ✓
- B) His humiliation on the cross
- C) His earthly ministry
- D) His birth in Bethlehem

**Question 9 (Short Answer)**
What is justification?
- **Expected Answer**: God declares us righteous based on Christ's righteousness imputed to us through faith. We are declared "not guilty" and accepted as righteous in God's sight.

**Question 10 (True/False)**
Reconciliation through Christ's atonement means the barrier between God and humanity is removed and we are restored to relationship with Him.
- **Answer**: True ✓

**Question 11 (Short Answer)**
What is redemption?
- **Expected Answer**: We are bought back from sin's slavery through Christ's payment. We are no longer slaves to sin but slaves to righteousness.

**Question 12 (Multiple Choice)**
How should the atonement affect the way believers live?
- A) It should motivate gratitude, obedience, and commitment to serving God ✓
- B) It means believers can sin without consequences
- C) It has no practical effect on daily living
- D) It means believers should become monks

#### Written Reflection Questions (2)

**Question 1**: Which theory of atonement (substitutionary, Christus Victor, moral influence, or satisfaction) most deeply impacts your personal faith and why?

**Question 2**: How does understanding Christ's atonement affect your sense of gratitude to God and your commitment to living for Him?

#### Written Assignment

**Comparative Analysis: Theories of Atonement (3-4 pages)**

Write a comprehensive analysis comparing and contrasting at least two theories of atonement (substitutionary, Christus Victor, moral influence, and satisfaction). Your analysis should include:

1. **Biblical Basis for Each Theory**: Provide Scripture references that support each theory you discuss.

2. **Strengths of Each Approach**: Explain what each theory emphasizes and why it is important for understanding the atonement.

3. **Weaknesses or Limitations**: Discuss any limitations or areas where each theory might be incomplete.

4. **How These Theories Complement One Another**: Explain how multiple theories together provide a more complete understanding of Christ's work.

5. **Personal Reflection**: Conclude with which understanding most deeply impacts your personal faith and why.

3. **Breaking Legal Ground**: What does it mean to break legal ground, and why is this step crucial?

### Lesson 3: Maintaining Freedom

#### Quiz Questions (12 Total)

**Question 1 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the danger of an "empty house" according to Matthew 12:43-45?
- A) A person without spiritual filling can become worse than before ✓
- B) Physical houses are spiritually dangerous
- C) Demons cannot return to a person
- D) Deliverance is permanent without effort

**Question 2 (Short Answer)**
What are the four primary spiritual disciplines for maintaining freedom?
- **Expected Answer**: Prayer, Scripture reading and meditation, worship and thanksgiving, and fellowship with other believers.

**Question 3 (True/False)**
Daily prayer is essential for maintaining spiritual freedom.
- **Answer**: True ✓

**Question 4 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the purpose of Scripture meditation in maintaining freedom?
- A) To renew the mind, strengthen faith, and provide spiritual nourishment ✓
- B) To memorize facts
- C) To gain intellectual knowledge
- D) To pass tests

**Question 5 (Short Answer)**
How does worship and thanksgiving contribute to maintaining deliverance?
- **Expected Answer**: Worship focuses our attention on God's greatness and goodness, thanksgiving acknowledges His faithfulness, and both strengthen our relationship with God and reinforce our freedom in Him.

**Question 6 (Multiple Choice)**
What role does fellowship with other believers play in maintaining freedom?
- A) It provides accountability, encouragement, and community support ✓
- B) It is optional
- C) It can hinder personal growth
- D) It is only for weak believers

**Question 7 (Short Answer)**
What does it mean to "grow in spiritual maturity"?
- **Expected Answer**: To develop deeper knowledge of God, greater obedience to His Word, increasing Christlikeness in character, and more effective service in His kingdom.

**Question 8 (True/False)**
Developing Christ-like character is essential for long-term spiritual freedom.
- **Answer**: True ✓

**Question 9 (Short Answer)**
What are some signs that a person might be experiencing relapse into bondage?
- **Expected Answer**: Returning to old thought patterns, increased temptation, isolation from community, neglect of spiritual disciplines, and feelings of oppression or heaviness.

**Question 10 (Multiple Choice)**
What should a person do if they recognize signs of relapse?
- A) Immediately seek help from a mature believer or spiritual leader ✓
- B) Ignore the signs and hope they go away
- C) Assume deliverance was ineffective
- D) Turn to worldly solutions

**Question 11 (Short Answer)**
How does accountability help maintain spiritual freedom?
- **Expected Answer**: Accountability provides external support, encouragement, and correction. It helps believers stay committed to spiritual disciplines and provides early warning of potential relapse.

**Question 12 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the ultimate goal of maintaining freedom?
- A) To grow continuously in Christ and become more like Him ✓
- B) To avoid all temptation
- C) To achieve perfection
- D) To never face struggles again

#### Written Reflection Questions (2)

**Question 1**: What spiritual disciplines do you currently practice? How might you strengthen or add to your spiritual practices to maintain freedom?

**Question 2**: Who in your life provides accountability and encouragement in your spiritual journey? How can you cultivate deeper relationships that support your spiritual growth?

#### Written Assignment

**Personal Spiritual Discipline Plan (2-3 pages)**

Develop a comprehensive personal spiritual discipline plan for maintaining freedom. Your plan should include:

1. **Prayer Practice**: Describe your current prayer life and how you will develop a more consistent prayer practice. Include specific times, places, and prayer focuses.

2. **Scripture Engagement**: Outline a plan for regular Scripture reading and meditation. Include which books or passages you will study and how you will apply them.

3. **Worship and Thanksgiving**: Describe how you will incorporate worship and thanksgiving into your daily life. Include specific practices or times.

4. **Community and Accountability**: Identify believers who will provide accountability and encouragement. Describe how you will maintain these relationships.

5. **Character Development**: Identify one or two areas of character development you want to focus on. Describe how you will pursue growth in these areas.

6. **Evaluation and Adjustment**: Describe how you will evaluate your progress and adjust your plan as needed.

7. **Commitment**: Conclude with a personal commitment to maintaining your spiritual disciplines and seeking ongoing growth in Christ.
