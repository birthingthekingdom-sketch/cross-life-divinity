## EMAIL BEST PRACTICES:

### Do:
✅ Personalize with pastor's name  
✅ Keep emails concise and scannable  
✅ Include clear call-to-action  
✅ Provide multiple contact options  
✅ Use urgency appropriately (deadlines)  
✅ Include social proof (testimonials)  
✅ Make unsubscribe easy (if using email service)  

### Don't:
❌ Send too many emails (max 1 per week)  
❌ Use manipulative tactics  
❌ Ignore responses  
❌ Send generic mass emails  
❌ Forget to proofread  
❌ Overwhelm with too much information  

---

## TRACKING METRICS:

Monitor these metrics for each email:
- Open rate (aim for 30%+)
- Click-through rate (aim for 5%+)
- Response rate
- Conversion rate (enrollments)
- Unsubscribe rate (keep below 1%)

Adjust your approach based on what's working!

## FOLLOW-UP CALL SCRIPT:

If emailing doesn't get a response, consider a friendly phone call:

**Opening:**
"Hi [Pastor Name], this is [Your Name] from Cross Life School of Divinity. I wanted to follow up on the presentation from [date]. Do you have a quick minute?"

**If Yes:**
"Great! I wanted to see if you had any questions about the courses or the special offer. What would be most helpful for you to know?"

**If Interested:**
"Wonderful! Let me walk you through the enrollment process real quick. It'll take about 2 minutes..."

**If Not Interested:**
"I completely understand. Can I ask what would make this more valuable for you in the future? Your feedback helps us serve pastors better."

**Closing:**
"Thank you for your time, Pastor [Name]. I'll keep you updated on new courses and future offers. Blessings to you and your ministry!"

---

**Remember:** The goal is to build relationships, not just make sales. Be genuine, helpful, and respectful of their time and decisions.
