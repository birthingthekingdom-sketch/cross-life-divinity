# Cross Life School of Divinity - Lesson Enhancement Guide

## Overview

This guide provides comprehensive theological content, reading assignments, and quiz questions for enhancing all lessons across the Cross Life School of Divinity curriculum. Each lesson has been designed to meet the following standards:

- **Comprehensive Theological Content**: Detailed, academically rigorous material grounded in Scripture and theological tradition
- **Reading Assignments**: Primary texts, supplementary readings, and Scripture references
- **Minimum 10 Quiz Questions**: A mix of multiple-choice, true/false, and short-answer questions
- **1-2 Written Reflection Questions**: Designed to promote deeper engagement and personal application

## Course Enhancement Structure

### DIV101: Foundational Theology

#### Lesson 1: Introduction to Systematic Theology

**Content Focus:**
- Definition and purpose of systematic theology
- Historical development from medieval period through Reformation
- Key characteristics: coherence, comprehensiveness, biblical foundation, practical application
- Six major doctrinal categories
- Relationship between faith and reason

**Reading Material:**
- **Primary**: Grudem (Chapters 1-3), Calvin (Institutes, Book I, Chapters 1-5)
- **Supplementary**: Erickson, Bloesch
- **Scripture**: 2 Timothy 2:15, Romans 12:2

**Assignment:**
- 2-3 page personal theological reflection
- Written questions on personal application and doctrinal importance

**Quiz Questions (12 total):**
1. Primary focus of systematic theology (MC)
2. Medieval theologian reconciling faith and reason (MC)
3. Meaning of "sola scriptura" (MC)
4. Doctrinal categories (MC - identify non-example)
5. Faith and reason relationship (MC)
6. Doctrine coherence (T/F)
7. Purpose of studying systematic theology (SA)
8. Goal of systematic theology (MC)
9. Definition of Theology Proper (SA)
10. How theology should transform believers (MC)
11. Scripture as ultimate authority (T/F)
12. Difference between biblical and systematic theology (SA)

## Quality Standards

### Content Standards

- **Theological Accuracy**: All content grounded in Scripture and mainstream Christian theology
- **Academic Rigor**: Appropriate for seminary-level study
- **Accessibility**: Clear language for diverse learners
- **Comprehensiveness**: Addresses major themes and concepts

### Assessment Standards

- **Question Variety**: Mix of multiple-choice, true/false, and short-answer
- **Difficulty Progression**: Questions progress from basic recall to application
- **Answer Clarity**: Correct answers are unambiguous and defensible
- **Learning Alignment**: Questions assess stated learning objectives

### Assignment Standards

- **Clear Instructions**: Students understand what is expected
- **Appropriate Length**: Assignments are proportionate to course level
- **Grading Rubrics**: Clear criteria for evaluation
- **Reflection Depth**: Written work promotes critical thinking
