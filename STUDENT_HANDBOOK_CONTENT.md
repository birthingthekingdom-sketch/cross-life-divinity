# Cross Life School of Divinity - Student Handbook

## Welcome to Cross Life School of Divinity

Welcome to the Cross Life School of Divinity (CLSD) community! ## Getting Started: Your First Steps

### Creating Your Account

Your journey at Cross Life School of Divinity begins with account creation. Visit our website and select "Sign Up" to create your student account. You will need to provide your full name, email address, and create a secure password. We recommend using a strong password that includes uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters to protect your account.

After registration, you will receive a welcome email confirming your account creation. Check your email (including spam folders) for this important message, which contains your login credentials and a link to access our learning platform.

### Logging In to Your Account

Once your account is created, you can log in at any time using your email address and password. Simply visit the login page, enter your credentials, and click "Log In." You will be directed to your personal student dashboard, which displays your enrolled courses, progress tracking, upcoming assignments, and important notifications.

If you forget your password, click "Forgot Password?" on the login page. Enter your email address, and we will send you a secure password reset link. Follow the instructions in the email to create a new password. For security reasons, password reset links expire after 24 hours, so act quickly if you need to reset your password.

### Enrollment Options

Cross Life School of Divinity offers flexible enrollment options to meet your needs and budget. You can choose from individual course purchases, structured learning paths, course bundles, or our all-access subscription. Each option is designed to provide excellent value and support your theological education goals.

**Individual Courses:** Purchase individual courses at $89 per course with lifetime access. This option is perfect if you want to study specific topics without committing to a full program. Once you purchase a course, you retain access indefinitely and can study at your own pace.

**Learning Paths:** Our three structured learning paths ($199 each) guide you through a carefully sequenced curriculum designed for specific educational goals. The Beginner Path introduces foundational theology and discipleship skills. The Intermediate Path builds advanced theological knowledge and ministry competencies. The Advanced Path prepares you for specialized ministry roles and leadership positions. Each path includes 4-6 courses organized in a logical progression with prerequisites to ensure you build knowledge systematically.

**Course Bundles:** Select any three courses and save $88 with our course bundle option ($299 total). This option provides flexibility while offering significant savings compared to individual course purchases.

**All-Access Subscription:** Our most popular option, the all-access subscription ($49/month) provides unlimited access to all 18 courses and 180+ lessons. With no course limits and the ability to study at your own pace, this option offers exceptional value for students pursuing comprehensive theological education.

**Payment Plans:** All programs are available with 0% interest payment plans spread over six months, making theological education more accessible. Choose to pay in full or spread your investment across manageable monthly payments.

### Enrollment Verification

After completing your payment, you will receive an enrollment confirmation email. To unlock full course access, you must complete our enrollment verification process. This involves submitting a government-issued photo ID (driver's license, state ID, or passport) through our secure upload form. This verification process typically takes 2-3 business days.

During this verification period, you have immediate access to course materials for preview purposes. Once your ID is verified and approved, you gain full access to all course content, quizzes, assignments, and the ability to earn certificates.

## Navigating Your Student Dashboard

### Dashboard Overview

Your student dashboard is your personal command center at Cross Life School of Divinity. Upon logging in, you will see your personalized dashboard displaying your enrolled courses, progress tracking, upcoming assignments, and important announcements.

The dashboard is organized into several key sections. Your course cards show all enrolled courses with visual progress indicators. Each course card displays the course title, number of lessons, your current progress percentage, and quick action buttons to continue learning or view course details.

Your progress summary provides an overview of your overall achievement, including total courses enrolled, courses completed, average quiz scores, and CLAC hours earned. This summary helps you track your overall progress toward your educational goals.

Upcoming assignments and due dates are prominently displayed to help you stay organized and meet deadlines. You can click on any assignment to view details, access submission instructions, and submit your work.

### Accessing Your Courses

To access a course, click on the course card from your dashboard or navigate to the "My Courses" section. Each course page displays the course description, learning objectives, total lessons, and a complete lesson list. Click on any lesson to begin studying.

Lessons are organized sequentially, and you can access them in any order. However, some courses may have prerequisite requirements that you must complete before accessing advanced lessons. These prerequisites are clearly indicated on the course page.

### Tracking Your Progress

Your progress is automatically tracked as you complete lessons, take quizzes, and submit assignments. The dashboard displays your completion percentage for each course, helping you visualize your progress toward course completion.

You can view detailed progress analytics by clicking "My Progress" in your dashboard. This page shows your completion rates by course, quiz score trends, assignment grades, study time tracking, and CLAC hours earned. These analytics help you identify areas where you are excelling and areas where you might benefit from additional focus.

## Understanding Course Structure

### Lessons and Learning Materials

Each course consists of 10 comprehensive lessons designed to build your knowledge progressively. Each lesson includes multiple components to support your learning.

Lesson content provides the core educational material, typically 3,000-4,500 words of seminary-quality theological instruction. Content includes opening statements that frame the lesson topic, extensive Scripture passages with verse-by-verse commentary, Greek and Hebrew word studies for deeper linguistic understanding, theological analysis connecting biblical concepts to contemporary ministry, and practical applications helping you implement what you learn in your ministry context.

Most lessons include an introductory video providing visual context and instructor commentary. These videos range from 5-15 minutes and complement the written lesson content. You can watch videos directly on the lesson page or download them for offline viewing.

Reading materials and Scripture references are provided for each lesson. We encourage you to engage with the primary sources and recommended theological resources to deepen your understanding. Many lessons include links to free online resources and references to classic theological works.

### Quizzes and Assessments

Each lesson includes a comprehensive quiz designed to assess your understanding of the material. Quizzes typically contain 10 questions in multiple formats: multiple-choice questions testing factual knowledge and comprehension, true/false questions assessing your understanding of key concepts, and short-answer questions requiring you to apply concepts to ministry scenarios.

Quizzes are automatically graded for multiple-choice and true/false questions, providing immediate feedback. Short-answer questions are reviewed by our instructors, who provide detailed feedback within 2-3 business days. You must score at least 70% on quizzes to earn credit for course completion.

You can retake quizzes as many times as needed to achieve the 70% passing grade. We encourage you to review the lesson material before retaking quizzes to improve your understanding and performance.

### Assignments and Written Work

Most courses include written assignments requiring you to apply course concepts to your ministry context. Assignments typically ask you to write 500-1,500 word papers on topics related to the lesson content, develop ministry plans or strategies based on course concepts, or create reflective essays connecting biblical principles to contemporary ministry challenges.

Assignments are submitted through our online submission system. You can upload Word documents, PDFs, or other file formats. Each assignment has a clear due date, and you can submit early or request extensions if needed. Assignments are graded by our instructors using a detailed rubric assessing theological understanding (40%), content quality and depth (40%), and writing mechanics and clarity (20%).

You will receive detailed feedback on your assignments within 5-7 business days of submission. If you do not achieve the required grade, you can revise and resubmit your assignment for reconsideration. This revision process allows you to improve your work and deepen your learning.

## Academic Policies and Standards

### Grading System

Your performance in each course is evaluated based on multiple components. Quiz scores typically comprise 50% of your course grade, reflecting your understanding of lesson content. Assignment grades comprise 40% of your course grade, reflecting your ability to apply concepts and communicate theological understanding. Participation and engagement comprise 10% of your course grade, reflecting your active involvement in course discussions and forums.

Your final course grade is calculated as a weighted average of these components. You must achieve a minimum grade of 70% to pass a course and earn credit. Grades are calculated automatically and displayed on your dashboard.

### Passing Requirements

To earn credit for a course and be eligible for a CLAC certificate, you must achieve a minimum grade of 70% in the course. This grade reflects successful mastery of course content and achievement of learning objectives.

If you do not achieve a 70% grade on your first attempt, you can retake quizzes and resubmit assignments to improve your grade. We encourage you to review the material, seek clarification on concepts you find challenging, and utilize available resources to support your learning.

### Certificate Eligibility

Upon completing all lessons, quizzes, and assignments in a course with a minimum grade of 70%, you become eligible to earn a CLAC certificate for that course. Certificates are automatically generated and available for download from your dashboard.

CLAC certificates include your name, the course title, the date of completion, CLAC hours earned, a unique verification code, and a QR code for verification. These certificates represent your achievement and can be shared with employers, educational institutions, or used for professional development documentation.

### Incomplete Grades and Extensions

If you are unable to complete a course by the scheduled end date, you can request an extension. Extensions are typically granted for up to 30 additional days. To request an extension, contact our student support team with an explanation of your circumstances.

If you do not complete a course within the extension period, your enrollment may be marked as incomplete. Incomplete enrollments do not count toward course completion, but you retain access to course materials and can complete the course at any time.

## Student Conduct and Academic Integrity

### Code of Conduct

As a member of the Cross Life School of Divinity community, you are expected to conduct yourself with integrity, respect, and professionalism. This includes treating other students and instructors with respect, following all platform rules and policies, using the platform only for authorized educational purposes, and refraining from any behavior that disrupts the learning environment.

Violations of our code of conduct may result in warnings, temporary suspension of account access, or permanent removal from the platform, depending on the severity of the violation.

### Academic Integrity

Academic integrity is fundamental to our mission and values. We expect all students to complete their own work, properly cite sources, and refrain from plagiarism, cheating, or other forms of academic dishonesty.

Plagiarism includes submitting work written by others without attribution, copying text from sources without quotation marks and citations, paraphrasing sources without citation, and submitting the same work in multiple courses without permission. If plagiarism is detected, your assignment will not receive credit, and you may be required to revise and resubmit the work with proper citations.

Cheating includes unauthorized collaboration on quizzes or assignments, using unauthorized resources during quizzes, sharing quiz answers with other students, and submitting work that is not your own. Cheating violations may result in course failure and removal from the platform.

We use plagiarism detection software to identify potential academic integrity violations. If violations are detected, we will contact you to discuss the situation and determine appropriate next steps.

### Respectful Communication

We are committed to creating an inclusive, welcoming community where all students feel respected and valued. We expect all communication to be respectful, professional, and free from discrimination, harassment, or hate speech based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics.

If you witness or experience disrespectful communication or harassment, please report it to our student support team immediately. We take all reports seriously and will investigate promptly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do I have to complete a course?**
You have unlimited time to complete courses. There are no time limits or deadlines for course completion. You can study at your own pace and take as long as you need to master the material.

**Can I access courses on mobile devices?**
Yes, our platform is fully responsive and works on smartphones, tablets, laptops, and desktop computers. You can access courses from any device with an internet connection.

**What if I fall behind in my studies?**
If you fall behind, contact our student support team. We can help you create a catch-up plan, connect you with academic support resources, and provide encouragement and accountability to help you get back on track.

**Can I download course materials for offline viewing?**
Yes, you can download lesson content, videos, and reading materials for offline viewing. This is especially helpful if you have limited internet connectivity or prefer to study offline.

**How do I earn CLAC certificates?**
CLAC certificates are automatically generated when you complete all lessons, quizzes, and assignments in a course with a minimum grade of 70%. Certificates are available for download from your dashboard immediately upon completion.

**Can I transfer credits to other institutions?**
While CLSD is CLAC-accredited, individual institutions determine their own transfer credit policies. Many accredited institutions accept CLAC credits from non-accredited schools. We recommend contacting your target institution directly to inquire about their transfer credit policies.

**What if I need to cancel my subscription?**
You can cancel your subscription at any time through your account settings. Cancellation takes effect at the end of your current billing period, and you retain access to all courses until that date.

**How do I update my payment method?**
You can update your payment method through your account settings. Click "Payment Methods" and follow the prompts to add or update your payment information.

**Can I get a refund?**
We offer a 14-day money-back guarantee on all purchases. If you are not satisfied for any reason, contact our student support team within 14 days for a full refund.

**How do I contact student support?**
You can contact our student support team through our contact form at support@crosslifeschoolofdivinity.org, by phone at (312) 300-3295 (Monday-Friday, 9am-5pm CST), or through our live chat feature available on the website.
