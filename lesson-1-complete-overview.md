### 2. Lesson Content (Comprehensive Exegetical Study)

#### I. The Great Commission - Exegetical Study
- **Full Scripture Text**: Matthew 28:18-20 (NKJV) written out completely
- **Verse-by-Verse Commentary**:
  - Verse 18: Greek word study on ἐξουσία (exousia) - "authority"
  - Verse 19: Analysis of πορευθέντες (poreuthentes) - grammatical structure
  - Verse 20: Study of τηρεῖν (terein) - "to observe/keep"
- **Historical Context**: Post-resurrection authority validation
- **Theological Significance**: Universal scope of the gospel

#### II. The Gospel Message - Romans Road
- **Romans 3:23**: Full text with Greek analysis
  - ἁμαρτάνω (hamartanō) - "to miss the mark"
  - ὑστερέω (hystereō) - "to come short"
- **Romans 6:23**: Contrast study
  - ὀψώνια (wages) vs χάρισμα (gift)
  - Three types of death explained
- **Romans 5:8**: Substitutionary atonement
  - ὑπέρ (hyper) - "for/in place of"

#### III. Personal Testimony Structure
- Three-part framework:
  1. Before Christ
  2. Conversion
  3. After Christ
- Biblical examples provided (Paul, Samaritan woman, demoniac)

#### IV. Practical Application
1. Prayer for the lost
2. Preparation through study
3. Proclamation with boldness
4. Patience in God's timing

#### V. Advanced Theological Concepts
- **Doctrine of Calling (Vocatio)**:
  - External call (general invitation)
  - Internal call (effectual work of Holy Spirit)
- **Regeneration vs. Conversion**:
  - Regeneration: God's sovereign act
  - Conversion: Human response

#### VI. Historical Context
Early church evangelistic methods:
- House to house (Acts 5:42, 20:20)
- Public proclamation (Acts 17:16-34)
- Synagogue reasoning (Acts 17:2-3)
- Marketplace dialogue (Acts 17:17)

#### VII. Reading Requirements
**Primary Texts**:
- Matthew 28:16-20 (in Greek if possible)
- Acts 1:8
- Romans 1:16-17
- 2 Corinthians 5:14-21

**Recommended Scholarly Resources**:
- D.A. Carson, "Matthew" in *The Expositor's Bible Commentary*
- John Stott, *Christian Mission in the Modern World*
- Will Metzger, *Tell the Truth: The Whole Gospel to the Whole Person*

### 5. Lesson Quiz (10 Advanced Questions)

**Sample Questions**:

1. **Greek Grammar**: "In Matthew 28:18, the Greek word ἐξουσία (exousia) refers to:"
   - Answer: Delegated authority and right to rule
   - Explanation provided

2. **Exegetical Analysis**: "The main imperative verb in the Great Commission is:"
   - Answer: Make disciples (μαθητεύσατε)
   - Grammatical explanation included

3. **Theological Concepts**: "According to Romans 6:23, the Greek word χάρισμα means:"
   - Answer: Free gift
   - Contrasted with "wages"

4. **Biblical Languages**: "The term πάντα τὰ ἔθνη (panta ta ethne) refers to:"
   - Answer: All people groups/ethnic groups
   - Cultural and missiological implications

5. **Systematic Theology**: "The doctrine of 'effectual calling' teaches that:"
   - Answer: The Holy Spirit internally draws the elect to salvation
   - Biblical support provided

6. **Apologetics**: "According to 1 Peter 3:15, believers should give a defense with:"
   - Answer: Gentleness and respect
   - Greek term ἀπολογία explained

7. **Atonement Theology**: "The substitutionary atonement in Romans 5:8 is indicated by:"
   - Answer: ὑπέρ (hyper) - for/in place of
   - Theological significance explained

8. **Biblical Theology**: "Which is NOT one of the three types of death in Romans 6:23?"
   - Answer: Intellectual death
   - Correct types: physical, spiritual, eternal

9. **Biblical Hermeneutics**: "Paul's method in Acts 17:2-3 involved:"
   - Answer: Reasoning from the Scriptures
   - Apologetic methodology

10. **Greek Etymology**: "The word ἁμαρτάνω (hamartanō) literally means:"
    - Answer: To miss the mark
    - Theological implications for hamartiology
